Consent to Treatment Policy
Purpose of This Policy The purpose of this policy is to ensure that all patients at Sheikh Zayed Central Hospital understand their medical treatments and give informed consent before any procedure or care is provided.
What Is Informed Consent? Informed consent means that patients have been clearly informed about:
  • The nature and purpose of the treatment or procedure.
  • The potential risks and benefits involved.
  • Alternative options that may be available.
  • The possible results of refusing treatment.
The patient must agree voluntarily, without pressure or misunderstanding.
When Consent Is Required
  • Before any surgery, operation, or invasive procedure.
  • Before receiving anesthesia or sedation.
  • Before starting new medication or therapy with possible side effects.
  • Before participating in research or experimental treatments.
Types of Consent
  • Verbal Consent: Given verbally for simple or low-risk treatments.
  • Written Consent: Signed by the patient for surgeries or higher-risk procedures.
  • Implied Consent: When a patient agrees through actions, such as extending an arm for a blood test.
Who Can Give Consent
  • Adult patients (18 years or older) who are mentally capable of making decisions.
  • Parents or legal guardians for patients under 18 years old.
  • Next of kin or legally authorized representatives if the patient is unable to consent.
Right to Refuse Treatment Every patient has the right to refuse treatment after being informed of the consequences. The hospital will respect this decision and document it properly.
Emergency Situations In emergencies where immediate treatment is necessary to save a life or prevent serious harm, consent may be assumed if the patient cannot communicate and no guardian is available.
Language and Understanding Medical staff must use clear, simple language when explaining treatments. Translators are available if the patient does not understand the language used.
Withdrawal of Consent Patients have the right to withdraw their consent at any time before or during treatment. Medical staff will explain the possible results of this decision.
Confidentiality All consent forms and discussions are kept private and stored securely in the patient’s medical record.
Final Note Sheikh Zayed Central Hospital is committed to ensuring that every patient gives informed, voluntary, and well-understood consent for all medical care.
